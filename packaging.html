05. Founder GTM Recommendation

Executive brief on code focus priorities and core outcome positioning.

BUILD FIRST (CORE VALUE)

  • Patient CRM Loops

    Design automatic engagement cycles tracking chronic patient milestones.

  • Follow-Up Engine

    Establish diagnostic timelines to trigger follow-up alerts automatically.

  • WhatsApp Layer

    Enable friction-free patient responses directly inside WhatsApp chat screens.

  • Mobile Experience

    Design clinical consoles optimized for high-speed mobile interfaces.

  • AI consultation Notes

    Implement background voice transcribers to write intake details automatically.

  • AI Revenue Recovery

    Create procedural code audit checklists to flag missed procedure charges.

DO NOT BUILD INITIALLY (BLOAT Voids)

  • Hospital ERP Systems

    Avoid bed-allocations, complex scheduling, and ward logs.

  • Inventory Systems

    Avoid stock lists, wholesale ordering pipelines, and batch-expiry tracking.

  • Laboratory Systems (LIS)

    Skip analyzer device integrations; ingest and parse reports from clean PDF streams.

  • Complex Compliance Products

    Avoid multi-regional regulatory architectures; maintain local invoicing frameworks.

  • Pharmacy ERP Systems

    Do not develop retail stock ledgers, barcode scanners, or distributor lists.

GO-TO-MARKET GOLDEN RULE

"DO NOT SELL SOFTWARE. SELL OUTCOMES."

Doctors Do Not Buy Features:

  • Appointments Calendar
  • Billing & Receipt grids
  • Patient Record files
  • Data entry pages
➔

Doctors Buy Business Outcomes:

  • More Revenue (Recapture missed slots)
  • More Repeat Patients (Automated checks)
  • Less Staff Dependency (Autonomous bots)
  • Less Administrative Work (Voice drafting)
  • More Time (Consultations complete 75% faster)
  • Better Patient Retention (Seamless WhatsApp)

"The biggest opportunity is not another clinic management software. The opportunity is an AI-powered Clinic Operating System that actively helps clinics retain patients, recover revenue, automate operations and grow."
